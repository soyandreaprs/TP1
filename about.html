Statement

Lidia's practice is built upon drawing as a fundamental structure—a language through which she explores the tension between the organic and the constructed. Through angular, fragmented lines, Lidia composes visual fields that evoke memory maps, inner landscapes, or contemporary ruins. Fragmentation, layering, and geometric abstraction create shifting spaces where materiality and the intangible coexist.
Her work operates through contrasts: between solidity and fragility, the revealed and the concealed. Lidia uses layers of matter and techniques such as scalpel incisions to erode or unveil surfaces, generating textures that encapsulate traces of time. Black plays a central role—not merely as an outline, but as a structural presence that organizes the composition and intensifies expressive gesture.
Whether in black and white or in dialogue with color, Lidia's work reflects on space as a fragmented construction, charged with visual memory and latent energy.
